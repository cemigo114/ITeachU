# HSN-Q.A.1 · High School · Number and Quantity/Quantities
## "The Commuter's Calculation"
*Use units as a way to understand problems and to guide the solution of multi-step problems; choose and interpret units consistently in formulas; choose and interpret the scale and the origin in graphs and data displays.*

---

## 1. Student Prompt
"My older brother is trying to figure out if it's cheaper to take the city bus or drive his old truck to his new job 12 miles away. The bus is $2.25 per ride, but his truck gets 18 miles per gallon and gas costs $3.60 per gallon right now. If he has all these different numbers like 'miles,' 'dollars per gallon,' and 'miles per gallon,' how can he mash them together to find out the actual cost for just one trip?"

---

## 2. Possible Misconceptions

1. **Operation Confusion** *(Type: procedural_overgeneralization)* Students might multiply the distance by the fuel efficiency (12 x 18) instead of dividing, resulting in "square miles per gallon" rather than gallons.

2. **Unit Neglect** *(Type: surface_feature_matching)* Students may simply add all the numbers provided (2.25 + 12 + 18 + 3.60) because they see them all as part of the same "cost" problem.

3. **Incomplete Scaling** *(Type: partial_concept_model)* Students might calculate the cost of one gallon of gas but forget to factor in that the trip only uses a fraction of a gallon.

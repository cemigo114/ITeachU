# 7.EE.A · Grade 7 · Expressions and Equations
## "The Discount Dilemma"
*Apply properties of operations as strategies to add, subtract, factor, and expand linear expressions with rational coefficients. Understand that rewriting an expression in different forms in a problem context can shed light on the problem and how the quantities in it are related.*

---

## 1. Student Prompt
My friend and I are looking at these skateboards that are normally $x$ dollars, but the shop is having a "20% off everything" sale. I think the price is just $x - 0.20x$, but my friend says it’s way easier to just calculate $0.80x$. If we both start with the same original price, are we actually going to end up with the same total at the register?

---

## 2. Possible Misconceptions

1. **Additive vs. Multiplicative Confusion** *(Type: additive_vs_multiplicative_confusion)* The student tries to subtract the 20% as a flat 20 cents ($x - 0.20$) rather than 20% of the variable $x$.
2. **Term Detachment** *(Type: structural_misunderstanding)* The student treats the $0.20x$ as a separate entity and fails to realize that $x$ has an implicit coefficient of 1.
3. **Operational Misinterpretation** *(Type: misinterpretation_of_representation)* The student believes that $0.80x$ represents an 80% discount rather than the remaining 80% of the original cost.

---

## 3. Pattern Recognition Prompts

- If the skateboard was exactly $100, what happens to the numbers in my way of doing it versus my friend's way?
- Does that "0.20" part always have to be attached to the $x$, or can it just hang out by itself?
- Why does my friend's version only have one term while mine has two?

---

## 4. Generalization Question (Always / Sometimes / Never)
"Wait, so if you're taking a percentage off, is it ALWAYS the same as just multiplying by whatever percentage is left over, or does that only work for certain numbers?"

---

## 5. Inference and Prediction
So I just saw a sign for a different shop that says "Buy 3 skateboards, get 10% off the total." I was thinking the total would be $(x + x + x) - 0.10(x + x + x)$, but then I saw a skater write down $0.90(3x)$ on his hand. Do you think those two ways are actually talking about the same amount of money? And if I wanted to buy 5 boards instead, is there a way to know the total without writing out the whole long subtraction thing?

**Prediction target:** The simplified decimal coefficient for the total cost of 5 skateboards with a 10% discount.

---

## 6. Mapping and Process Data

### Claims
- The student can apply properties of operations to add, subtract, and expand linear expressions with rational coefficients.
- The student can interpret how different forms of an expression represent the same relationship between quantities in a context.

### Evidence
Evidence is provided when a student demonstrates that $1x - 0.20x$ simplifies to $(1 - 0.20)x$ using the distributive property. Deep reasoning is shown when the student explains that $0.80x$ represents the "remaining portion" while $x - 0.20x$ represents the "original minus the discount," proving they see the mathematical structure behind the context.

### Process Data Revealing Critical Thinking
A student demonstrates high-level thinking by self-correcting the "invisible 1" coefficient in front of a variable when combining like terms. This shift from seeing $x$ as a symbol to seeing $x$ as $1x$ allows them to bridge the gap between two-term subtraction and single-term multiplication.

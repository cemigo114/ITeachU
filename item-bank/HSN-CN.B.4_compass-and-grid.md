# HSN-CN.B.4 · High School · Number and Quantity/The Complex Number System
## "The Compass and the Grid"
*(+) Represent complex numbers in rectangular and polar form (including real and imaginary numbers), and explain why the rectangular and polar forms of a given complex number represent the same number.*

---

## 1. Student Prompt
"I found two different ways to describe where the point $3 + 3i$ sits on a map—one person says to just go '3 right and 3 up,' but another person says you just have to turn $45^\circ$ and walk a certain distance straight from the center. If they both end up at the exact same physical dot on the paper, is there a way to describe that 'walking distance' using the numbers we already have?"

---

## 2. Possible Misconceptions

1. **Angle-Only Definition** *(Type: partial_concept_model)* Students might think the angle ($45^\circ$) is enough to define the number, forgetting that infinite points exist along that same line at different distances.
2. **Component Addition** *(Type: additive_vs_multiplicative_confusion)* Students may believe the distance from the center is simply $3 + 3 = 6$ instead of using the Pythagorean relationship.
3. **Rectangular-Polar Disconnect** *(Type: surface_feature_matching)* Students might view $3 + 3i$ and its polar equivalent as two different numerical values rather than two different "addressing systems" for the same spot.

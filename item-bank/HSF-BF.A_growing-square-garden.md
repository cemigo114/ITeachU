# HSF-BF.A · High School · Functions/Building Functions
## "The Growing Square Garden"
*[HSF-BF.A.1] Write a function that describes a relationship between two quantities.*

---

## 1. Student Prompt
"I'm helping my neighbor build a square garden that starts out 2 feet by 2 feet, but every year she adds exactly 1 foot to the length and 1 foot to the width. I'm trying to figure out how many square feet of dirt we'll need for the floor in 10 years, but do I have to draw the bigger square for every single year to find the total space?"

---

## 2. Possible Misconceptions

1. **Linear Growth Assumption** *(Type: additive_vs_multiplicative_confusion)* Students might notice the side length increases by 1 each year and assume the total area also increases by a constant amount each time.

2. **Incorrect Starting Value** *(Type: partial_concept_model)* Students may forget to include the original 2x2 area and only calculate the area of the "new" border added each year.

3. **Input-Output Misalignment** *(Type: structural_misunderstanding)* Students might use the side length as the "year number," forgetting that at Year 0 the side is already 2 feet long.

---

## 3. Pattern Recognition Prompts

- "If we look at how much the area grows from Year 1 to Year 2, is that the same amount it grows from Year 2 to Year 3?"
- "What is the one thing about the shape that stays exactly the same every single time it gets bigger?"
- "How does the number of years we've waited connect back to how long just one side of the square is?"

---

## 4. Generalization Question (Always / Sometimes / Never)
"Wait, so if the sides are growing by a steady amount, does that mean the total area is ALWAYS going to grow by a steady amount too, or does it start to accelerate the longer we wait?"

---

## 5. Inference and Prediction
"Okay, so what if she decided to make it a rectangular garden instead, starting at 2 feet by 5 feet, but she still only adds 1 foot to both sides every year? I noticed the area in Year 1 was 18, and in Year 2 it was 28... do you think we can find a trick to get the area for Year 50 without having to do all the years in between?"

**Prediction target:** Determining the area for a high-value year by establishing a relationship between the year and the dimensions $(2+n)$ and $(5+n)$.

---

## 6. Mapping and Process Data
### Claims
- Students can construct a functional relationship (recursive or explicit) that models the area of a shape based on a change in its dimensions over time.
- Students can differentiate between linear changes in dimensions and the resulting non-linear change in area.

### Evidence
Evidence is gathered when a student recognizes that the side length is $(n + 2)$ and the area is $(n + 2)^2$. The task design forces this by moving the student from simple addition to a multiplicative relationship that varies based on the input year.

### Process Data Revealing Critical Thinking
Critical thinking is revealed when a student stops adding "strips" of area to the previous year's total and instead switches to multiplying the current year's dimensions. This shows a shift from recursive thinking to building an explicit functional model.

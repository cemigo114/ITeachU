# 6.NS.C · Grade 6 · The Number System
## "The Submarine & The Skyscraper"
*Apply and extend previous understandings of numbers to the system of rational numbers.*

---

## 1. Student Prompt
"I'm looking at a map of a coastal city. The top of a skyscraper is at 450 feet above sea level, but a research submarine is at 450 feet *below* sea level. My friend says they are at the 'same number' because they both say 450, but I feel like one of them should have a minus sign. If we use 0 to represent the surface of the ocean, how do we show that they are in totally opposite directions, and what does it mean if the sub moves to $-500$ feet?"

---

## 2. Possible Misconceptions

1. **Absolute Value Confusion** *(Type: partial_concept_model)*
Students may think that $-500$ is "bigger" than $-450$ because 500 is a larger digit, failing to realize that $-500$ represents a position that is *lower* (less) on the vertical number line.

2. **Zero as 'Nothing'** *(Type: overgeneralization_of_prior_knowledge)*
Students often struggle to see 0 as a specific location (sea level, freezing point) rather than just "the absence of value."

3. **Directional Neglect** *(Type: surface_feature_matching)*
A student might describe the submarine's depth as "450" and the skyscraper's height as "450," losing the critical distinction between positive and negative elevation in a mathematical model.

---

## 3. Pattern Recognition Prompts

- "If you are at 0 and you go up 10 feet, you are at 10. If you go down 10 feet from 0, where are you?"
- "Wait—if a fish is at $-10$ feet and a shark is at $-50$ feet, which one is actually deeper in the water?"
- "Does the minus sign tell you *how much* distance there is, or does it tell you *which way* you went from zero?"

---

## 4. Generalization Question (Always / Sometimes / Never)
"I have a hunch that if you have two numbers like $10$ and $-10$, they are ALWAYS the exact same distance away from zero, just in different directions... or is there a number where the 'negative version' is further away?"

---

## 5. Inference and Prediction
"I found a treasure chest at $-600$ feet and a sunken ship at $-300$ feet. If the submarine is currently at $-450$ feet, do you think it needs to go *up* or *down* to reach the ship? And like, if the temperature outside the sub drops from $2^\circ\text{C}$ to $-5^\circ\text{C}$, is it getting warmer or colder?"

**Prediction target:** Identifying that $-300 > -450$ (moving up) and that $-5 < 2$ (getting colder).

---

## 6. Mapping and Process Data

### Claims
- Students can use positive and negative numbers to represent quantities in real-world contexts (elevation, temperature, debt).
- Students can identify that a number and its opposite are equidistant from zero on a number line.

### Evidence
Evidence is gathered when a student correctly labels the submarine's position as $-450$ and explains that $-500$ is "less than" $-450$ because it is deeper. The task forces this by using a vertical context (sea level), which naturally maps to the vertical axis of a coordinate system.

### Process Data Revealing Critical Thinking
A student shows deep understanding when they distinguish between the *value* of the number (position) and the *absolute value* (distance). Self-correction occurs when a student realizes that "the submarine went down" means the number became smaller, even though the digits got larger.

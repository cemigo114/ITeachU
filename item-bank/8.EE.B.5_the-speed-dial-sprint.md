# 8.EE.B.5 · Grade 8 · Expressions and Equations
## "The Speed-Dial Sprint"
*Graph proportional relationships, interpreting the unit rate as the slope of the graph. Compare two different proportional relationships represented in different ways.*

---

## 1. Student Prompt
I’m trying to figure out who’s faster at this new typing game—my friend says she can do 120 words in 2 minutes, but the leaderboard just shows a straight line on a graph that hits the point (5, 250). Since their numbers are in different places, is there a way to tell who’s actually winning?

---

## 2. Possible Misconceptions

1. **Coordinate Confusion** *(Type: misinterpretation_of_representation)* The student treats the (5, 250) point as a simple total rather than a ratio of 250 words per 5 minutes.
2. **Steepness Misinterpretation** *(Type: surface_feature_matching)* The student assumes a "higher" number like 250 automatically means a faster rate without comparing the time intervals.
3. **Unit Rate Neglect** *(Type: procedural_overgeneralization)* The student tries to compare the raw numbers 120 and 250 directly without finding out how many words each person types in exactly 1 minute.

---

## 3. Pattern Recognition Prompts

- If we look at the 120 words in 2 minutes, what would happen to that number if we only let her type for 1 minute?
- On that leaderboard graph, if the line is perfectly straight, what does that tell us about how many words are added every single minute?
- How does the "steepness" of the line on the graph connect back to the actual speed of the typing?

---

## 4. Generalization Question (Always / Sometimes / Never)
"Wait, so if a graph starts at (0,0) and is a straight line, does that mean the slope is ALWAYS the same thing as the unit rate, no matter which point on the line we pick to check?"

---

## 5. Inference and Prediction
I found a third player who uses a table to track their speed, and it shows they did 180 words in 3 minutes. If we put all three players on the same graph, do you think any of their lines would actually overlap, or would one be way steeper than the others?

**Prediction target:** Which player's line would be the steepest and how many words they would finish if the game lasted for 10 minutes.

---

## 6. Mapping and Process Data
### Claims
- The student can calculate and compare unit rates from different representations (textual descriptions, graphs, and tables).
- The student can interpret the slope of a line in a proportional relationship as the unit rate of the scenario.

### Evidence
The task requires students to convert the friend's verbal rate and the leaderboard's graphical point into a common unit (words per minute). Evidence of deep reasoning is demonstrated when a student explains that the point (5, 250) represents a slope of 50, which is faster than the friend's slope of 60—or realizes that the friend is actually faster despite the "smaller" numbers in her description.

### Process Data Revealing Critical Thinking
An observable behavior of deep understanding is when a student realizes that the "steepness" of the graph is the visual version of the "speed" in the word problem, showing they have connected the geometric concept of slope to the physical concept of rate.

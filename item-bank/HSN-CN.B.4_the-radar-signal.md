# HSN-CN.B.4 · High School · Number and Quantity
## "The Radar Signal"
*Represent complex numbers on the complex plane in rectangular and polar form (including real and imaginary numbers), and explain why the rectangular and polar forms of a given complex number represent the same number.*

---

## 1. Student Prompt
I saw a radar screen where a signal was at $3 + 4i$, but the operator said the target was actually "5 units away at a $53^\circ$ angle." If one person is talking about a grid and the other is talking about a distance and a turn, are they even looking at the same dot?

---

## 2. Possible Misconceptions

1. **Category Mismatch** *(Type: structural_misunderstanding)* The student believes that a "rectangular" address and a "polar" address describe two different physical locations rather than two ways to label one point.

2. **The Addition Trap** *(Type: additive_vs_multiplicative_confusion)* The student tries to find the distance by adding the coordinates ($3 + 4 = 7$) instead of using the geometric distance from the origin.

3. **Angle-Axis Confusion** *(Type: misinterpretation_of_representation)* The student assumes the angle is measured from the vertical imaginary axis instead of the horizontal real axis, leading to a "flipped" version of the point.

---

## 3. Pattern Recognition Prompts

- If we moved the dot further to the right on the grid, what would have to happen to that "5 units away" distance to keep up?
- Does the angle of the turn ever actually change if we keep the dot on the same diagonal line but just slide it further out?
- How does the "3" and the "4" on the grid connect to the "5" that the operator mentioned?

---

## 4. Generalization Question (Always / Sometimes / Never)
"Wait, so if I know the 'left-right' and 'up-down' steps for any complex number, does that mean I can ALWAYS use them to figure out exactly how far it is from the center and what angle it’s pointing at?"

---

## 5. Inference and Prediction
I found another signal at $-3 + 3i$ and the screen says it is "about 4.2 units away." If I wanted to tell the operator exactly which way to turn the radar dish to find it, do you think I could use the same triangle trick, and would the angle be more or less than a $90^\circ$ turn?

**Prediction target:** The specific angle (in degrees) and the exact distance (in radical form) for the complex number $-3 + 3i$.

---

## 6. Mapping and Process Data
### Claims
- The student can convert a complex number from rectangular form ($a + bi$) to polar form ($r(\cos\theta + i\sin\theta)$).
- The student can justify why two different mathematical representations (grid coordinates vs. magnitude and direction) identify the same point on the complex plane.

### Evidence
The task requires the student to reconcile the Cartesian coordinates (3, 4) with the polar magnitude (5) and direction ($53^\circ$). Evidence of deep reasoning is provided when a student uses the Pythagorean theorem and trigonometry to demonstrate that $\sqrt{3^2 + 4^2} = 5$, proving the two descriptions are mathematically identical.

### Process Data Revealing Critical Thinking
An observable behavior of deep understanding is when a student realizes that the "real" part corresponds to $x$ and the "imaginary" part corresponds to $y$, allowing them to apply right-triangle geometry to a number system that previously seemed abstract.

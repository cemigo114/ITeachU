# HSA-REI.C · High School · Algebra/Systems of Equations
## "The Phone Plan Cross"
*Solve systems of equations.*

---

## 1. Student Prompt
"I'm looking at two different monthly phone plans where one charges a flat $30 fee plus $2 for every gigabyte of data, and the other is just a flat $50 with unlimited data. If my bill came out exactly the same for both last month, how much data did I actually use?"

---

## 2. Possible Misconceptions

1. **The Single Variable Trap** *(Type: partial_concept_model)*
Students might try to solve for the cost or the data separately without realizing both variables must be equal at the point of intersection.

2. **Subtraction Confusion** *(Type: additive_vs_multiplicative_confusion)*
Students may attempt to subtract the $30 fee from the $2 rate rather than finding the difference between the two flat fees.

3. **Misinterpretation of 'Unlimited'** *(Type: surface_feature_matching)*
Students might struggle to represent a flat rate as an equation because it lacks a visible "rate" or variable term.

---

## 3. Pattern Recognition Prompts

- "Wait—does the $50 plan ever change price if I use 1GB or 100GB of data?"
- "If I use 5GB, which plan is cheaper, and does that switch at some point?"
- "How much 'extra' money is the second plan charging upfront compared to the first one's flat fee?"

---

## 4. Generalization Question (Always / Sometimes / Never)
"Wait, so if two plans have different starting fees and different rates per gigabyte, is there ALWAYS going to be a specific amount of data where they cost the exact same?"

---

## 5. Inference and Prediction
"My sister found a third plan that's $10 a month but $4 per gigabyte, and she says it's way better for her. If she ends up paying the same $50 as the unlimited plan, do you think we can find her data usage using the same logic we used for my plan? Or does the higher rate change the 'trick' for finding the crossover point?"

**Prediction target:** The specific data usage (gigabytes) where the third plan equals the cost of the flat-rate plan.

---

## 6. Mapping and Process Data

### Claims
- Students can represent a system of linear equations from a contextual scenario and identify the significance of the intersection point.
- Students can utilize algebraic methods (substitution or elimination) to find the unique solution that satisfies multiple constraints simultaneously.

### Evidence
Evidence is provided when a student sets the two cost expressions equal to one another to find the specific 'x' value. The task forces students to move beyond calculating individual costs to finding the relational "break-even" point where the two mathematical models overlap.

### Process Data Revealing Critical Thinking
Critical thinking is revealed when a student explains that the solution represents the only point where the choice between the two plans does not matter financially.

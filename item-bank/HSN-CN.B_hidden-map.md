# HSN-CN.B · High School · Number and Quantity/The Complex Number System
## "The Hidden Map"
*Represent complex numbers and their operations on the complex plane.*

---

## 1. Student Prompt
"I found this old math puzzle that says complex numbers like $3 + 2i$ aren't just symbols on a page, but actually live on a 'hidden' map where the regular numbers go left-to-right and the $i$ numbers go up-and-down. If $3 + 2i$ is just a single point on this grid, what happens to that point's location if we add another number like $1 - 4i$ to it?"

---

## 2. Possible Misconceptions

1. **One-Dimensional Thinking** *(Type: overgeneralization_of_prior_knowledge)* Students may try to plot the number on a standard number line by adding the coefficients (e.g., $3 + 2 = 5$) and ignoring the directional nature of $i$.

2. **Origin-Anchor Confusion** *(Type: partial_concept_model)* Students might think the "plus" sign in $3 + 2i$ means you start at 3 and move 2 units, but fail to see the resulting $a + bi$ as a single coordinate $(a, b)$.

3. **Scalar Addition Error** *(Type: additive_vs_multiplicative_confusion)* Students may treat the addition of $1 - 4i$ as a scaling factor (stretching the point) rather than a translation (sliding the point) across the plane.

---

## 3. Pattern Recognition Prompts

- "If you only changed the 'real' part of the number, which way would the point on the map actually slide?"
- "Wait—does the $i$ part of the number always control the vertical 'up and down' movement, or can it move sideways too?"
- "When you add two complex numbers together, is the new spot on the map just the result of two separate 'slides'?"

---

## 4. Generalization Question (Always / Sometimes / Never)
"I have a hunch that adding a complex number to another one is ALWAYS just like following a set of 'walking directions' (like 3 steps east, 2 steps north)... does that mean the order you add them in doesn't change where you end up on the map?"

---

## 5. Inference and Prediction
"I noticed that if I have the number $2 + 2i$ and I multiply the whole thing by $-1$, the point jumps to the exact opposite side of the map at $-2 - 2i$. If multiplying by $-1$ is like a half-turn around the center, do you think there’s a way to figure out what multiplying by just $i$ does to the point's location without just guessing?"

**Prediction target:** The geometric rotation of a point in the complex plane when multiplied by $i$.

---

## 6. Mapping and Process Data

### Claims
- Students can represent complex numbers as points or vectors in the complex plane.
- Students can interpret the addition of complex numbers geometrically as a translation or vector sum in the plane.

### Evidence
Evidence is observed when students describe the sum of two complex numbers as a "move" or "slide" corresponding to the real and imaginary components. The task forces deep reasoning by linking abstract algebraic addition to spatial movement, requiring students to coordinate two independent dimensions simultaneously.

### Process Data Revealing Critical Thinking
A student demonstrates critical thinking when they stop treating $a$ and $bi$ as terms to be simplified into a single value and start treating them as independent horizontal and vertical components of a single position.

# HSN-RN.A · High School · The Real Number System
## "The Rapidly Shrinking Block"
*Extend the properties of exponents to rational exponents.*

---

## 1. Student Prompt
"So I've been watching this simulation of a block that shrinks by half every minute, but then I started wondering about what happens right in the middle of a minute. If it starts at 64 cubic inches and it's 32 after one minute and 16 after two minutes, is there a way to know exactly how big it is at exactly one minute and thirty seconds without just guessing it's halfway between 32 and 16?"

---

## 2. Possible Misconceptions

1. [cite_start]**Linear Interpolation Bias** *(Type: additive_vs_multiplicative_confusion)* The student assumes the size at 1.5 minutes is the arithmetic mean (24) because they are applying a constant rate of change instead of a constant percent rate. [cite: 363]
2. **Root Index Confusion** *(Type: misinterpretation_of_representation)* The student recognizes a root is involved but struggles to identify that the denominator of the rational exponent is the root index.
3. [cite_start]**Procedural Overgeneralization** *(Type: procedural_overgeneralization)* The student attempts to divide the base by the exponent or multiply the base by the fraction instead of using properties of exponents. [cite: 189]

---

## 3. Pattern Recognition Prompts

- "Wait—if we look at the size every full minute, does the amount it loses stay the same or is it changing?"
- "Okay, but is there a specific multiplier that carries us from one minute to the next every single time?"
- "If we had to take two 'mini-steps' to get from 32 to 16 using the exact same multiplier twice, what would that number even have to be?"

---

## 4. Generalization Question (Always / Sometimes / Never)
"Wait, so if a block is shrinking by half every minute, does that mean the multiplier for a 'half-minute' step is ALWAYS going to be the square root of 0.5?"

---

## 5. Inference and Prediction
"I just found this other simulation where a bacteria colony triples every hour! It starts with 10 bacteria, then 30 after one hour, then 90 after two. I'm curious if we can use that same 'split-step' logic to figure out how many there are at 20 minutes, since that's exactly one-third of an hour."

**Prediction target:** The number of bacteria present at the 40-minute mark (two-thirds of an hour).

---

## 6. Mapping and Process Data

### Claims
- Students can extend properties of integer exponents to justify the interpretation of fractional exponents.
- Students can rewrite expressions involving radicals and rational exponents using the properties of exponents. [cite: 189, 204]

### Evidence
The task design forces students to find a value between two known integer exponents, which requires them to move beyond whole number bias. Evidence of mastery is shown when a student explains that the multiplier for a partial interval is the $n$-th root of the full interval's multiplier.

### Process Data Revealing Critical Thinking
Deep understanding is revealed when a student self-corrects from an additive 'halfway' point (24) to a multiplicative one ($\approx 22.6$) by testing if applying their chosen multiplier twice correctly results in the next full-minute value. [cite: 182]

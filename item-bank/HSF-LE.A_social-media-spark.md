# HSF-LE.A · High School · Functions/Linear and Exponential Models
## "The Social Media Spark"
*Distinguish between situations that can be modeled with linear functions and with exponential functions.*

---

## 1. Student Prompt
"I posted a video yesterday and noticed that every hour, exactly 50 more people had viewed it than the hour before. But my friend says their video is 'going viral' because their views are actually doubling every single hour. Since we both started with 10 views, is there a point where my steady growth eventually falls behind their doubling speed?"

---

## 2. Possible Misconceptions

1. **Growth Rate Confusion** *(Type: additive_vs_multiplicative_confusion)* Students may think that adding 50 is "faster" than doubling simply because 50 is a larger number than 2.
2. **Initial Lead Bias** *(Type: surface_feature_matching)* Students might conclude the linear model is superior because it has more views in the first few hours (60 vs 20).
3. **Linearity of Percentage** *(Type: partial_concept_model)* Students may incorrectly assume that "doubling" (100% growth) means adding the same amount of views each time.

---

## 3. Pattern Recognition Prompts

- "When you look at your views from Hour 1 to Hour 2, is that 'jump' the same size as the jump from Hour 10 to Hour 11?"
- "Wait—if your friend's views keep doubling, does the amount they add each hour stay the same or does that number change?"
- "How does the 'starting amount' affect your steady $+50$ growth versus your friend's $\times 2$ growth?"

---

## 4. Generalization Question (Always / Sometimes / Never)
"Wait, so if one thing adds a fixed amount and another thing multiplies by a fixed number, is the multiplying one ALWAYS going to end up with a bigger total if we just wait long enough?"

---

## 5. Inference and Prediction
"I found a third video where the views are growing by 10% every hour instead of doubling. It feels super slow right now because it's only adding like one view at a time, but do you think it acts more like my '+50' video or my friend's 'doubling' video? Like, if we checked back in a month, would that tiny 10% jump eventually catch up to my steady 50-view hourly gain?"

**Prediction target:** Identifying that any constant percent growth (exponential) will eventually exceed any constant additive growth (linear).

---

## 6. Mapping and Process Data
### Claims
- Students can distinguish between situations where a quantity grows by a constant real number versus a constant percentage/factor.
- Students can demonstrate that an exponential function will eventually exceed a linear function.
### Evidence
Evidence is gathered when a student compares the differences between successive terms in the linear model (constant 50) and the ratios of successive terms in the viral model (constant factor of 2). The task design forces this by providing a scenario where the linear model is initially more successful, requiring the student to project growth over time to see the intersection.
### Process Data Revealing Critical Thinking
Deep understanding is revealed when a student realizes that the "amount added" in the exponential model is itself growing, whereas the "amount added" in the linear model is stagnant. This realization marks the transition from calculating specific values to understanding the nature of the growth functions.

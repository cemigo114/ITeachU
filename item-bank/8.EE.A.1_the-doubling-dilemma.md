# 8.EE.A.1 · Grade 8 · Expressions and Equations
## "The Doubling Dilemma"
*Know and apply the properties of integer exponents to generate equivalent numerical expressions.*

---

## 1. Student Prompt
I was looking at this pattern where a cell doubles every hour, so after 3 hours there are $2^3$ cells, and I wondered what happens if you multiply that by another $2^2$ cells. Does that mean you have $4^5$ cells now because you doubled the bottom and added the top, or is that not how it works?

---

## 2. Possible Misconceptions

1. **Base Multiplier Error** *(Type: additive_vs_multiplicative_confusion)* The student multiplies the bases together (2 times 2) while also adding the exponents, resulting in $4^5$ instead of $2^5$.

2. **Exponent Multiplication** *(Type: procedural_overgeneralization)* The student multiplies the exponents (3 times 2) instead of adding them, leading to an answer of $2^6$.

3. **Linear Addition Bias** *(Type: whole_number_bias)* The student treats the entire expression as a simple addition problem ($2^3 + 2^2$) rather than recognizing the multiplicative nature of exponential growth.

---

## 3. Pattern Recognition Prompts

- If you wrote out all the "2s" being multiplied in $2^3$ and then wrote out all the "2s" in $2^2$ right next to them, what do you notice about the total count?
- When the number of hours changes, does the "doubling" part of the math ever actually turn into "quadrupling"?
- How does the total number of "2s" in the long string connect back to the little numbers at the top?

---

## 4. Generalization Question (Always / Sometimes / Never)
"Wait, so if the big numbers at the bottom are the same, is it ALWAYS true that you can just add the tiny numbers at the top to find the total, even if the exponents are negative?"

---

## 5. Inference and Prediction
I found this other weird case where a scientist had $2^8$ bacteria but then it got divided by $2^3$ because of some medicine. If multiplying them made the exponent bigger, do you think dividing them just makes the exponent smaller in the same way?

**Prediction target:** The resulting exponent when $2^8$ is divided by $2^3$, and whether the base stays a 2.

---
## 6. Mapping and Process Data
### Claims
- The student can apply the Product of Powers property ($a^m \cdot a^n = a^{m+n}$) to generate equivalent expressions.
- The student can justify why the base remains constant when multiplying powers with like bases.

### Evidence
The task requires students to expand the exponential notation into repeated multiplication to verify their logic. Deep reasoning is evidenced when a student explains that multiplying $2^3$ by $2^2$ is simply a string of five 2s, which forces the realization that the base cannot become a 4.

### Process Data Revealing Critical Thinking
An observable behavior of deep understanding is when a student self-corrects from $4^5$ to $2^5$ by physically or mentally counting the factors and realizing that "doubling" is the constant rule regardless of how many groups are combined.
